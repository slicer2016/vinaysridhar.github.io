Why I Created Lemma

I believe IRL conversations have untapped potential. In my life, the most fun discussions usually center around hairy, complicated situations someone is facing that doesn't have clear answers. Someone shares their multilemma (not dilemmas or trilemmas), and we collectively navigate the gray areas – it's a lot of fun and often leads to real connections.

That's why I created Lemma – with ideas from friends (and a few from LLMs!). It's a game that turns life's messiest moments into catalysts for meaningful conversation. No right answers, just real ones.

Currently in Beta, but if you're interested in being part of our journey, sign up below.

About The Game

What is Lemma?

Life isn't black and white. It's technicolor chaos. Lemma turns life's messiest moments into your next game night obsession. Each card presents a sticky situation with no "right" answer - just your answer.

How to Play

  1. Draw a situation card
  2. Everyone votes secretly
  3. Reveal & discuss (or argue)
  4. Repeat until you've solved all of life's problems (or run out of snacks)

Sample Scenarios

Work

Your team member copied code from a competitor's private repository. The feature is live and earning praise. No one will ever know. What do you do?

Community

You win $10,000. Your dog needs expensive surgery, but the local shelter desperately needs funding. What do you spend it on?

Family

Your parents create a family WhatsApp group including your aunt who constantly shares medical misinformation. Your sister (a doctor) leaves the group. Mom asks you to convince her to return. Your aunt has been lonely since her husband passed. What do you do?

Friendship

Your close friend is becoming a wellness influencer, sharing questionable health advice. As a doctor, you totally disagree. They just credited your friendship for "inspiring" their journey. Your colleague follows them. What do you do?

Romance

While helping your partner's recently widowed parent with their phone, you discover they have an active dating profile - just 3 months after the funeral. Your partner often mentions how their parent is "still grieving." Do you say anything?
