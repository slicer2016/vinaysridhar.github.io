Book Reviews

This is a collection of reflections of some of the books I've enjoyed over the years. I primarily write these notes to help recall ideas; they are not intended to be a holistic review of the book. This is a hangover from an older habit where I wrote notes in physical books as a time-capsule of where I was when I was reading a book (like this one from 2014 of Following Fish by Samanth Subramaniam).

My non-fiction reading is usually about art and culture, history, india, memoirs, science and nature. I also regularly read fiction, but do not usually write such reviews. Each of these were written right after I read the book and capture what I thought at the time, not necessarily what I think about these subjects today.

I also subscribe to Teju's Cole idea that the distinction between fiction and non-fiction is an arbitrary construction, but hey - it does have its uses.
